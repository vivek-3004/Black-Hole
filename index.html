The Secrets of Black Holes

Black holes are among the most mysterious and fascinating objects in the universe. They are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing—not even light—can escape.

1. Formation & Stellar Types

A black hole is formed when an enormous amount of mass is compressed into a very small space. Most are born from the explosive deaths of massive stars.

Stellar

Formed from collapsing stars.

Intermediate

Formed through mergers.

Supermassive

Found at galactic centers.

2. Event Horizon & Singularity

Event Horizon

The "Point of No Return". The boundary where escape velocity exceeds light speed. This is what creates the dark "shadow" we observe in telescope images.

Singularity

The core of infinite density where known laws of physics break down. A bridge between general relativity and quantum mechanics.

3. Imaging Breakthroughs

In 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) revealed the first image of a black hole in M87. In 2022, we successfully imaged Sagittarius A* at our own galaxy's center.
